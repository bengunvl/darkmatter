Without independent verification
  • You trust the system that ran the agent to also report on it
  • A record can be edited, backdated, or fabricated — and you won't know
  • In a dispute, you have logs. Your counterpart has the same logs. Neither proves anything.
  • Auditors and regulators can only evaluate what you give them — there's no independent reference point
With DarkMatter
  • Records are captured with a clear proof level, so you know exactly how much trust is still required
  • Signed records cannot be altered without detection
  • Records with independent proof can be verified without a DarkMatter account or internet connection
  • Proof level tells you exactly how much you can rely on a record
Proof Level

How provable is your record?

Not every record carries the same weight. DarkMatter makes that visible — and lets you verify it yourself.

Level What it means How you get it
Recorded DarkMatter captured this record. You can inspect it, but some trust in capture is still required. Proxy or browser extension — no code changes needed.
Signed The agent signed this record before it reached DarkMatter. It cannot have been changed in transit without detection. SDK — one extra line when your agent commits.
Sealed The record was signed and encrypted before leaving your system. DarkMatter never saw the content. SDK with your own encryption key.
Some records can be verified independently. Others require trust in how they were captured. DarkMatter shows you the difference.
Most teams start with Recorded and move to Signed as their agents mature. Sealed is for situations where the content must never leave your control.

How it works

Three steps. Works alongside your existing stack.

1
Record each action
Call dm.commit() after each action. The record is signed and stored outside your system.
ctx = dm.commit(payload={"input": x, "output": y, "model": "claude-opus-4-6"}, parent_id=prev_id)
2
Inspect or replay from any point
Walk the full chain, branch from any step, or compare two branches. The original record is never changed.
chain = dm.replay(ctx_id)   fork = dm.fork(ctx_id)   diff = dm.diff(a_id, b_id)
3
Export and verify independently
Export a self-contained package with all records and a standalone verifier. Anyone can check it — no account, no internet, no DarkMatter.
bundle = dm.export(ctx_id)  # → proof_bundle.zip + verifier.py
What DarkMatter does

Three things. No monitoring, no model lock-in, no workflow replacement.

DarkMatter records what your agents did, lets you inspect and replay it, and lets you prove it to anyone. It does not run your agents, manage your prompts, or judge whether the output was good.
