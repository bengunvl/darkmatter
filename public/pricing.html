Common questions

Things people ask before signing up.

Why is verification free on every plan?

Because gating verification by plan would mean independent proof is a premium feature. That breaks the independence guarantee. Anyone — including a third party who isn't a DarkMatter customer — should be able to verify a record. That's the point.

Why no per-seat pricing?

AI agents don't have seats. Per-seat pricing made sense when a human sat in front of a screen and clicked buttons. When agents make decisions at machine speed, the unit that matters is agent action volume — commits, not users. Pricing scales with what your agents actually do.

What counts as a commit?

Every call to POST /api/commit counts as one commit, regardless of payload size. Read operations (replay, verify, pull, export) and fork operations do not count toward your limit.

What happens when I hit a limit?

We notify you at 80% usage. Commits over the monthly limit return a 429 response. We do not silently drop data. Upgrade anytime to restore access immediately.

Can I self-host DarkMatter?

Yes. DarkMatter is fully open source under the MIT license. Deploy it with Node.js and Postgres inside your own infrastructure. All features included. Note that self-hosted deployments don't carry the independent audit trail guarantee — since you control the infrastructure, the independence guarantee requires an external host.

What is the retention minimum for EU AI Act compliance?

EU AI Act Article 19 requires logs be kept for at least 6 months. The Free plan retains for 30 days, which is below that threshold. Pro (90 days) and Enterprise (custom, up to 1 year and beyond) are designed for teams with compliance requirements. Retention policy is configurable per agent on Enterprise.

What is Context Passport?

Context Passport is DarkMatter's canonical schema for AI agent context handoffs — a structured, model-agnostic format any LLM or framework can produce and consume, with built-in cryptographic integrity, agent attribution, and lineage tracking. It's an open standard included on all plans. Learn more at contextpassport.com.
